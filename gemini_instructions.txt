== THAI LAWS ==

Question: Is cannabis legal in Thailand?
Answer: Cannabis in Thailand is classified as a medicinal plant, not a drug. However, starting June 26, 2025, its sale is permitted for medical purposes only. This means a valid prescription from a licensed doctor is required to purchase cannabis flowers. Getting such a prescription is very easy — come to any WeedeN store, tell the budtender you need a prescription, and they will arrange everything for you on the spot.

Question: Who can buy cannabis at WeedeN?
Answer: Only adults aged 20 and older. Sales are prohibited to pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and monks. An identity document is checked upon entry to the store.

Question: Is it allowed to smoke cannabis in the street or in public places?
Answer: No. Consuming cannabis in public places is prohibited by law. The fine is up to 25,000 baht under the Public Health Act. Consume only in private spaces.

Question: Can cannabis be taken out of Thailand or brought into the country?
Answer: No. Transporting cannabis across a national border — both entering and leaving — is strictly prohibited.

Question: Is it allowed to drive after consuming cannabis?
Answer: Absolutely not. Driving under the influence is illegal and life-threatening.

Question: Is a prescription required to purchase cannabis?
Answer: Yes, a valid prescription from a licensed specialist is required to purchase cannabis flowers. WeedeN helps obtain a prescription right in the store — licensed doctors work at our clinical locations.

Question: Who can issue a cannabis prescription?
Answer: A prescription can be issued by a licensed doctor. The good news is that WeedeN stores have doctors who will conduct a quick consultation and issue a prescription on the spot. Just come in and the budtender will arrange everything.

Question: How long is a prescription valid?
Answer: A prescription issued through a WeedeN clinic is generally valid for 4 days. The purchase must be made within that period.

Question: What are indica, sativa, and hybrid?
Answer: Indica — more commonly associated with a relaxing, body-centered effect, suitable for evening rest. Sativa — described as more uplifting and energetic, with some noting a boost in creativity. Hybrid — combines properties of both types; the effect depends on the specific strain and individual response.

Question: What are THC and CBD?
Answer: THC is the substance that produces the psychoactive effect ("high"). CBD is a non-psychoactive component, more commonly associated with relaxation, stress relief, and general well-being.

Question: How long does cannabis last?
Answer: It depends on the method of consumption. When smoking or vaping, the effect lasts 1–3 hours. Edibles can last up to 8 hours, with the effect possibly taking 30 minutes to 2 hours to onset.

== TIPS FOR BEGINNERS ==

Question: It's my first time. Where do I start?
Answer: The main rule — start slow and with small doses. When smoking: one small puff, wait 10–15 minutes before taking another. With edibles: start with a quarter of a gummy or a small portion, wait 30 minutes to 2 hours — the effect comes on slowly. Our budtenders will always help you find the right option.

Question: Does cannabis affect everyone the same way?
Answer: No. The effect varies greatly depending on the dose, tolerance, body weight, what you have eaten, and individual sensitivity. That is exactly why we recommend starting small.

Question: What should I do if I feel unwell after consuming?
Answer: Stop consuming immediately. Lie down, drink water, and try to calm down — the unpleasant sensations will pass. If your condition does not improve or you feel very unwell, seek medical assistance.

Question: Can cannabis be combined with alcohol or medications?
Answer: Cannabis can interact with prescription drugs, over-the-counter medications, and alcohol. If you are taking any medications, be sure to consult a doctor before consuming.
